Black people have always had it tough in America.


  • Slavery: Africans were brought from their home countries to work for white men for no pay at all. Slaves in the US built many prominent buildings, including but not limited to the White House, the Capital Building, and many buildings at the University of Virginia.
  • Surpression: After slavery was aboished in the US, black people were still not equal to white men. In the Constitution, black men were considered 3/5 of a man. Black people weren't allowed to do everything that white people could.
  • Modern Day: Today, African Americans are still at a disadvantage to white people. African Americans are having historically bad interactions with the law right now. They are also at a disadvantage when it comes to getting jobs and making schools.
